## What you'll need

Inside the box are three things: the yoyopod itself, a USB-C charging
cable, and a quick-start card. The card matters more than most quick-start
cards do — it carries the device's **claim code**, the short code you'll
use in a few minutes to connect the device to your family. Keep it
somewhere you'll find it again.

The SIM is already inside the device. There's nothing to insert, no tray
to open, and no plan to shop for before you begin — connectivity is part
of the product, not homework.

Beyond the box, you'll want a power outlet or any USB power source for the
first charge, and a parent's phone within reach. Pairing comes right after
this page, and it goes fastest when the phone is already on the table.

## Steps

**Let the kid open the box.** This is their device, so let them peel the
seal and hold it first. What they're holding is deliberately simple: one
button on the side, one small screen — we call it the canvas — and nothing
to install, download, or sign into on the device itself.

**Plug it in before the big moment.** The yoyopod ships with a partial
charge, so give it a few minutes on the USB-C cable while the excitement
builds. This is also a good moment for the parent to install the yoyopod
app on their phone — you'll need it on the very next page.

**Power it on.** Press and hold the side button for a few seconds until
the canvas lights up. That press-and-hold is the first thing your kid will
learn about the device, and it's a preview of the whole interface: there
is only ever that one button (the full tour is at
[One Button: How Kids Use It](/families/using-the-button/)).

**Watch the canvas wake up.** On first boot the canvas says a short hello
and then walks itself to the Setup screen, where it shows the claim code —
the same short code printed on your quick-start card. The device now sits
patiently on that screen. It isn't broadcasting anything, it isn't
searching for your phone; it's simply showing its code and waiting for
your family to claim it.

**Hand it over to the app.** With the device showing its code, pick up the
phone and continue at
[The Parent App & Pairing](/families/parent-app-setup/). That page takes
you from "code on the canvas" to "paired, configured, and ready for a
kid" in about ten minutes.

## Tips

Charge before the reveal if you can. A gift moment that starts with
"please wait while it charges" is a small heartbreak that thirty minutes
of forward planning avoids entirely.

Make unboxing a family moment rather than a parent chore. There's nothing
technical to do on the device side — one button, one wheel of choices,
nothing to install — so the kid can genuinely drive from the first minute
while you narrate.

Keep the quick-start card and the box. The card carries the claim code,
and the box is your friend for the return window and warranty. A kitchen
drawer beats recycling for at least the first month.

Set expectations early, and happily: there's no camera, no browser, and no
app store on this device — by design, not by omission. Music and stories
live on the device itself and play without any internet at all. If
grandparents ask what it is, the honest answer is simple: it plays music
and stories, it talks to a short list of family, and that's the point.
More on the thinking behind that at [Privacy](/families/privacy/).

## Troubleshooting

**The screen stays dark after holding the button.** Almost always a charge
issue. Plug in the USB-C cable, wait twenty to thirty minutes, then press
and hold the side button again — and hold it a beat longer than feels
natural. A device that charges normally but never wakes is a support case,
not a puzzle for you to solve.

**It powers on but never shows the Setup screen.** If the canvas goes
straight to the home wheel instead of showing a claim code, the device is
already bound to a household — which is normal for a hand-me-down or a
resold device, because passing a yoyopod on is meant to be ordinary. The
previous family releases it from their yoyopod app; the device then wipes
its local data and returns to the Setup screen, ready to be claimed fresh
by yours. If you bought it new and it still skips Setup, contact support.

**The battery seems empty out of the box.** That's normal — devices ship
with a partial charge, and a long ride in a delivery van can run it low.
Plug it in first; nothing about first setup is harmed by starting from a
low battery.

**The box arrives damaged or incomplete.** Don't set the device up — an
unclaimed device is trivial to swap, a configured one slightly less so.
Use the support contact on the quick-start card (or the retailer you
bought from) and start the setup story with the replacement.

## Open questions

- **Cable-only, or charger brick in the box?** Cable-only is cheaper and
  greener and most homes have USB power; a brick guarantees the
  first-charge moment works everywhere. Decide before packaging is
  designed.
- **Adopt a SIM that's live out of the box, or activate connectivity
  during pairing?** Live-on-arrival makes the first ten minutes magical
  but means shipping active SIMs; activate-on-pairing is operationally
  safer but adds a step exactly where we want zero friction.
- **Print the claim code on the quick-start card, or keep it canvas-only?**
  The printed code rescues setup if a canvas is cracked in shipping, but a
  canvas-only code can rotate and is cleaner for resale. Adopt one; the
  pairing page's wording depends on it.
- **Gate setup on a minimum charge, or let it run on whatever's in the
  battery?** A charge gate prevents a dead device mid-pairing; no gate
  respects the family's moment. If adopted, the gate should be minutes of
  charging, not an hour.
