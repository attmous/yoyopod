## Our promises

- Parents see everything the device shares; nobody else does
- Local-first by design: music and stories live and play on the device, no account required to listen
- Only the whitelist can reach your kid — there is no public identity to find
- No camera, no browser, no app store: whole categories of risk removed in hardware
- Plain language always — if a parent can't understand a setting, it's our bug

## What data exists and where it lives

- The contact whitelist: created by parents, needed for calls and voice notes (storage TBD)
- Live-ish location: shared with the parent app when enabled (retention TBD)
- Voice notes and calls: what is transient and what is stored, for how long (TBD)
- The content library: on the device itself, working without internet
- Account and device data held by us to run the service (exact inventory TBD)

## What we never do

- No ads, no selling or sharing data with data brokers
- No feed, no follower counts, no engagement mechanics aimed at kids
- No listening in: the microphone serves calls and push-to-talk, nothing else
- No public profiles for kids, no discovery by strangers
- No dark patterns to keep kids on the canvas — the device is built to be put down
