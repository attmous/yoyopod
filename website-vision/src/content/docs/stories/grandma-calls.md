## The moment

- Six o'clock, every evening: Grandma calls Emil from two towns away — it is the fixed point of his day
- Scene beat: the device chimes gently, the Talk screen shows it is Grandma, Emil taps to answer
- Scene beat: the call itself — homework, the neighbor's dog, what's for dinner
- The counter-beat: earlier that afternoon, an unknown number tried the device — and on Emil's end, nothing happened at all
- Scene beat: Emil's parents never had to explain "don't answer strangers" — there is nothing to explain

## What yoyopod does

- Whitelist calls: only contacts a parent has approved can reach the device — everyone else simply never rings
- What answering looks like for a 7-year-old: one glance at the canvas, one tap
- The unknown caller from Emil's side: no missed-call anxiety, no blinking badge, no decision to make (how parents are informed, if at all, TBD)
- Grandma's side of it: she calls a normal number from her normal phone — no app, no account (exact mechanics TBD)

## Why it matters

- The pillar made concrete: the whitelist is not a filter Emil manages, it is a world with only family in it
- For Emil: a phone-shaped ritual with Grandma, without a phone
- For parents: the entire category of "who is contacting my child" is settled at setup time
- For Grandma: she is a first-class user of the product without ever touching it
