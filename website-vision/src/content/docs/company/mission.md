## The gap before the smartphone

- Kids ages 7-14 want independence before parents are ready to hand over a smartphone
- The choices today: nothing at all, or a full smartphone with everything that comes with it
- What parents actually need in that gap: reach the kid, know roughly where they are, let them have their music
- Why "the first device before a smartphone" names a category, not a competitor
- The one-liner in context: "Safer independence for kids. Peace of mind for parents. Before the smartphone."

## Our answer

- A screen-light, parent-managed audio companion — buyer is the parent, user is the kid
- The four V1 pillars: whitelist calls & voice messages, live-ish location, local-first music/audio, parent mobile app
- One small calm screen and one physical button — deliberately less, not a cut-down more
- Music and stories work with no internet: independence does not depend on coverage
- What the answer refuses to be — [What yoyopod Is Not](/company/what-we-are-not/)

## Who we are

- Software-engineer fathers in Stuttgart, Germany — building the device we wanted for our own kids
- Why engineers-as-parents shapes the product: we test on the actual users at our own dinner tables
- How the story shows up in the build: prototype hardware today (Raspberry Pi Zero 2W + PiSugar Whisplay HAT), product board later
- Where to follow the work: https://github.com/attmous/yoyopod and the as-built engineering docs (website/ in the repository)
- The principles that keep us honest — [Product Principles](/company/principles/)
